About the Crop:

Papaya (Carica Papaya) Pusa Delicious matures in 8 to 12 months from planting. It thrives in well-drained, fertile loamy soil with a pH of 6.0 to 7.0. Each acre can produce about 20 to 30 tons of papayas annually. The crop requires approximately 1,000 to 1,200 mm of water per year, with consistent moisture essential for optimal fruit development. Input costs, including seeds, fertilizers, irrigation, and labor, typically range from ₹80,000 to ₹1,20,000 per acre. With market prices of ₹20 to ₹40 per kg, net profit can range from ₹3,00,000 to ₹8,00,000 per acre, depending on yield and market conditions. Efficient management practices and favorable conditions can maximize both yield and profitability.

  • Soil Composition: Well-Drained Loamy Soil with Ph 6.0 to 7.0
  • Water need per Acre: 1000-1200 mm per acre annually
  • Potential Dangers: Papaya Mealybug, Oidium caricae, Asperisporium caricae
  • Fertilizers: Ammonium Nitrate (33-0-0), Muriate of Potash, Zinc Sulfate
  • Pesticides: Imidacloprid, Lambda-Cyhalothrin, Malathion
  • Profit Per Acre: ₹2,65,000 to ₹11,20,000 per acre annually
